Abel Tasman

The first known European explorer to reach New Zealand and the islands of Fiji and Van Diemen's Land.


Here is a timeline of Abel Tasman's life:

  • 1603 - Born in Lutgegast, Netherlands.
  • August 14, 1642 - His first voyage begins. Sailed for Mauritius from Batavia (now Jakarta).
  • November 24, 1642 - Sighted the west coast of Tasmania, north of Macquarie Harbour.
  • December 1, 1642 - Anchored to the North of Cape Frederick Hendrick just North of the Forestier Peninsula. Then landed in Blackman Bay.
  • December 13, 1642 - Sighted land on the north-west coast of the South Island, New Zealand, becoming the first European to sight New Zealand.
  • June 15, 1643 - Arrived back in Batavia.
  • 1648 - Fell into disgrace when, drunk, he attempted to hang two sailors for alleged disobedience.
  • 1653 - Retired. Stays in Batavia where he owned a substantial amount of land.
  • October 10, 1659 - Died in Batavia, survived by his second wife, Jannetje, and his daughter, Claesjen.
